Threading with tildas

 (require tilda) package: tilda

syntax

(~> expr clause ...)

 
clause = thread-option
  | (expr ...)
  | (pre-expr ... hole post-expr ...)
     
hole = ~
  | ~id
     
thread-option = #:with pat expr/hole
  | #:do (expr/hole ...)
  | #:as id
  | #:when expr/hole expr/hole
  | #:unless expr/hole expr/hole
     
expr/hole = (pre-expr ... hole post-expr ...)
  | (expr ...)
  | expr
Threads the expr through the next clause, then that clause through the one after it and so on. "Threads" here means that the expr will replace a hole, if there is one, in the clause. Clause with such replacement becomes the new expr to be threaded through the next clause. hole is any unbound identifier that starts with ~.

Examples:
> (~> '(1 2 3)
      (map add1 ~)
      (second ~)
      (* 2 ~))

6

> (~> "foo"
      (string->bytes/utf-8 ~)
      (bytes->list ~)
      (map (curry * 2) ~)
      (list->bytes ~))

#"\314\336\336"
